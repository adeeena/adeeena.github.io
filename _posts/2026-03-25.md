Weeks—no, months—this idea has been fermenting in the back of my skull since I moved in. Mostly because that "fairytale-like" sun, on the rare occasions it manages to pierce through the 400-kilometer-thick cloud layer here in Yvelines, has the audacity to blast right through my window. And where does it land? Straight into my two "super little eyes," obviously.

Up until now, it wasn't a critical failure, but with summer approaching and the sun rising earlier with increasing thermal intensity, I finally broke. During lunch break, I forced myself into some manual labor: window foiling.

The result? Even now, in the afternoon, the glare is so blinding it’s biblical. My room is currently drowning in a rainbow spectrum so intense, nobody has ever seen anything like it.
