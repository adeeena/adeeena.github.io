title: A horse has 9 months of pregnancy
---

Working with a handful of IT guys is like having a lifelong Leeloo Dallas Multipass to avoid ever having to eat lunch with colleagues. Even if you're forced into it, the conversation rarely scales beyond a binary assessment of whether the cheesy pasta is "decent" or "garbage."

However, even my off-the-charts antisocial self felt the need to simulate some team spirit for once. I wasn't looking for deep emotional bonding – god forbid – but I figured we could at least make a minimal effort to look like a coherent unit.

But as I was deconstructing my chocolate mousse, the conversation veered into a bizarre biological rabbit hole. They started brooding over how pregnant women can't eat raw eggs, and how lucky they are to be pregnant for only nine months—unlike whales, who carry for two years. That’s two years without sushi or mousse. Then came the "scientific" epiphany: how unique our nine-month term is in the animal kingdom, and how few other species share it. Like horses, for instance. Because horses also take exactly nine months. (Spoiler: they don't, but facts weren't invited to this lunch).

That was my cue. I grabbed my half-finished mousse, trudged back to the "awesome" open office, and finished my meal in silence. I sat there in deep regret, cursing the moment I ever thought eating with colleagues was a good idea.

Oh, and indeed I just had to change the USB port for the USB key to let the win7 installer continue its job. Admirable user experience.
